# Syntrixa Business Context

Purpose: provide the stable business model, positioning, target customer, offer, audit meaning, and delivery philosophy that all OpenClaw agents use when reasoning about Syntrixa work.

This file is business context only. It does not grant permission to execute, retrieve, store, contact, bill, deploy, or mutate anything. Use `TOOLS.md`, `MEMORY.md`, `ROUTING.md`, and `VALIDATION.md` for operational boundaries.

## Company Role

Syntrixa builds custom AI agents, workflow automation systems, and multi-agent orchestration solutions for businesses and individuals.

Syntrixa should be treated as a managed AI operations partner, not as a generic software vendor, template automation seller, or one-time development agency.

Primary job-to-be-done:

- evaluate client operations
- identify automation and orchestration opportunities
- design fit-for-purpose AI operational systems
- integrate with existing tools and workflows
- deploy safe, maintainable automation layers
- manage, improve, support, and modify deployed systems over time

## Core Offerings

Syntrixa solutions may include:

- AI agents
- multi-agent systems
- workflow orchestration systems
- business process automation
- operational automation
- custom integrations
- automation pipelines
- dashboards and management layers
- internal copilots
- managed AI operations
- maintenance, support, optimization, and modification of deployed systems

Solutions are customized according to client requirements, operational complexity, existing systems, business goals, integration constraints, and deployment risk.

## Target Customers

Primary target customers:

- small non-core tech businesses
- small agencies
- ecommerce product companies

Secondary fit:

- operations-heavy organizations
- founders and operators
- teams with repetitive workflows
- businesses with disconnected tools
- organizations seeking leaner operations and lower manual overhead
- companies with limited automation maturity

Long-term, the Syntrixa model can apply across industries where repeatable operations, coordination overhead, fragmented tooling, or process bottlenecks create automation opportunities.

## Problems Syntrixa Solves

Syntrixa is relevant when a business has:

- repetitive manual workflows
- operational inefficiencies
- disconnected systems
- scaling bottlenecks
- workflow complexity
- excessive manual coordination
- inefficient business operations
- fragmented tooling environments
- operational overhead
- repeatable processes that could be augmented or replaced by AI-enabled systems

Agents should reason in terms of operational improvement, automation maturity, feasibility, risk, and managed execution, not only feature delivery.

## Engagement Model

Typical lifecycle:

1. Lead
2. Discovery
3. Audit or planning
4. Contract
5. Build
6. Deploy
7. Managed monthly operations

Pricing model:

- planning, assessment, or audit phase
- one-time build and deployment cost
- recurring monthly management fee

Pricing depends on system complexity, orchestration complexity, integration requirements, operational scope, client requirements, support expectations, and risk.

Do not quote fixed prices unless n8n supplies approved pricing context. When pricing is requested without approved context, ask for scope context or route for human review.

## Audit Request Meaning

An audit request is not a generic contact inquiry.

An audit request means the client wants Syntrixa to evaluate business operations, workflows, systems, processes, and automation opportunities.

Audit or planning work may include:

- business analysis
- workflow mapping
- process evaluation
- automation opportunity identification
- orchestration planning
- inefficiency detection
- integration assessment
- implementation feasibility analysis
- solution architecture planning
- cost and complexity estimation
- deployment strategy planning

Audit outputs may include recommendations, implementation direction, proposed architecture, plans, engagement recommendations, and risk notes.

Implementation begins after contract agreement. Agents must not imply that an audit request automatically starts implementation.

## Managed Monthly Operations

Monthly management may include:

- monitoring
- maintenance
- bug fixing
- workflow optimization
- prompt tuning
- system improvements
- operational support
- analytics and reporting
- integration maintenance
- support and modifications

Minor maintenance and previously included capabilities may be covered when supplied policy context says so.

Significant new implementations, major features, expanded scope, or additional systems may require additional charges. If exact entitlement is unclear, ask for policy context or route to human review.

## Agent Reasoning Rules

All agents should:

- separate supplied facts, inferences, unknowns, and assumptions
- prefer operational diagnosis over generic sales language
- identify workflow, integration, orchestration, and managed-operations signals
- ask for missing scope, systems, volume, risk, timeline, and success criteria when needed
- route service/policy/pricing uncertainty to Helpdesk or human review when context is missing
- route public company/account evidence needs to Research
- route funnel, positioning, campaign, and outbound messaging work to Marketing and Sales
- route failures, incidents, retry safety, and monitoring issues to Health Monitoring
- never promise implementation, price, scope, timeline, or entitlement without approved context
